Design Goal


As the goal In-vehicle voice is not far from our lives. Nowadays, modern cars are basically equipped with in-car conversational agents. Since both hands and vision are occupied when driving, voice control can play a good role in driving. The current in-car conversational agent is relatively complete for basic functions: media, telephone, entertainment, Eastern Airlines system control, system query and so on. However, some problems existed: for example, the dialogue form is relatively rigid and the interaction is not natural enough. These problems make users need to have a certain understanding in advance to use the in-vehicle voice without obstacles. Therefore, this research aims to explore a more intelligent and natural way of interacting with the in-vehicle voice system in specific situations. Specifically, we analyze the current in-car conversational agents and give the solutions to overcome the existing limitations.
